Program: IT Assistant (Informationstechnischer Assistent) — Basic vocational training
Institution: BBS-ME Otto-Brenner-Schule, Hannover, Niedersachsen
Period: 2025–2026 (ongoing at time of documentation)

Context
This folder documents structural and didactic failures observed and formally raised during attendance at the BBS-ME Otto-Brenner-Schule. The documentation presented here is fact-bound, externally verified, and compiled in accordance with the same epistemological standards applied throughout this repository.
The exit grade reflects a disrupted learning environment — not technical capability. The core issues are not personal grievances but institutionally documented patterns, independently confirmed by external academic sources.

Documented Issues
1. Verified False Attribution — Leibniz Quote
A quote attributed to Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz was repeatedly used in classroom instruction:
"Nie haben die Menschen mehr Geist bewiesen, als bei den Spielen, die sie erfunden haben. Ganz allgemein gesprochen sind es immer die geistreichsten, die die Spiele erfinden und die Dümmsten, die diese Spiele am besten spielen."
This attribution was formally submitted to the Leibniz-Archiv, Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz Bibliothek (GWLB), Hannover for verification.
Response from Dr. Malte-Ludolf Babin, Leibniz-Forschungsstelle:
"Leibniz hat sich zwar mehrfach zum Thema Spiele ausgelassen, das von Ihnen beigebrachte Zitat dürfte allerdings nicht auf ihn zurückgehen."
The use of a false attribution in instruction — particularly one implying students who learn methodically are inferior — represents a failure of basic academic integrity in a teaching context.
See: 01_Leibniz_Falschzitat_Beleg.png

2. External Academic Confirmation — Prof. John Hattie
Structural concerns about the school's didactic approach were communicated directly to Professor John Hattie, Melbourne Laureate Professor Emeritus, University of Melbourne — the world's leading researcher in educational effectiveness (Visible Learning).
Hattie responded personally (17 November 2025):
"Yes so often students are bottom of the power ladder, and ironically schools should exist to build their learning power [...] By simply change the focus from How to Impact should lead to major improvements in our teaching." "Maintain the rage — John"
This response independently confirms the structural critique raised: the school's focus on performance-based testing over systematic knowledge building contradicts established evidence in educational science.
See: 02_Hattie_Anfrage.png / 02_Hattie_Antwort.png

3. Withheld Learning Resources — LPI Material
The LPI Linux Essentials learning material (LPI-Learning-Material-010-160-de.pdf, 6.24 MB) — a core reference for the IT program — was not introduced, distributed, or referenced by teachers during instruction.
A class poll conducted via the school platform (LARA) with 11 participants produced the following result:
    • 0% knew the material 
    • 100% had never encountered it 
    • 0% knew it before school 
This constitutes a documented failure to provide students with foundational professional resources in a program designed to qualify them for IT careers.
See: 03_LPI_Umfrage.png

4. Public Complaint — LARA School Platform
A formal written complaint was submitted via the school's official communication platform (LARA), addressed to fellow students, teachers, and school leadership. The complaint documented: missing structural knowledge transfer, false quote usage, psychological attribution of structural failures to individual students, and the contradiction between classroom practice and modern professional standards including AI literacy.
See: 04_LARA_Beschwerde.png

5. Independent External Confirmation — Public Reviews
Multiple independent reviews of the BBS-ME on public platforms document identical patterns, including teacher isolation from student reality, authoritarian classroom dynamics, and student powerlessness — consistent with the structural critique raised internally.
See: 05_Externe_Rezensionen.png
